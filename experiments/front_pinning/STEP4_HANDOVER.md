## What Step 4 Changes

### 1. The locked coarse-grid regime is lattice-pinned.

At D=0.12, dx=1.0 (coupling ratio 0.12), seeds ignite locally to U≈3
but the front cannot propagate to neighboring nodes. This holds across
both solvers and both boundary conditions.

Wider seeds do not rescue propagation. Even a 31-node seed (31 spatial
units) remains pinned at D=0.12, dx=1.0.

### 2. There is a sharp, reproducible pinning boundary.

| Threshold | Value | Confirmed by |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| D at dx=1.0 | between 1.50 and 2.00 | Euler+Heun, Periodic+Neumann |
| dx at D=0.12 | between 0.25 and 0.30 | Euler+Heun, Periodic+Neumann |
| Critical coupling ratio D/dx² | between 1.5 and 2.0 | 6 ratio tests, all consistent |
| Width rescue at locked params | NO (tested to 31 nodes) | Euler+Heun, Periodic+Neumann |
| Critical seed at fine grid (dx=0.10) | 7 nodes (0.7 spatial units) | Euler+Heun, Periodic+Neumann |

### 3. Earlier GRIMOIRE results remain valid IN THEIR OWN REGIME.

Gate1 (ignition AUC), Gate2B (structural outcomes), GSM seed scoring,
and local phase interleaving all test LOCAL dynamics. They do not depend
on global front propagation. The pinned regime is the correct regime for
those results.

### 4. The three terms of the equation have distinct, now-characterized roles.

- λU²sin(αU): creates the potential landscape (WHERE states exist)
- D∇²U: determines communication between nodes (WHETHER information propagates)
- Γ(U): external drive, still undefined (HOW the system is forced)

## What Step 4 Does NOT Yet Prove

### 1. This is a discretized-grid result, not a continuum theorem.

The pinning is a property of the lattice differential equation, not the
continuous PDE. The audit's Section 4 proves that in the continuum
limit, V(3)<V(1) guarantees front propagation. The pinning exists only
because the discrete Laplacian creates Peierls-Nabarro barriers.

Safe: "In the discretized finite-grid implementation, the locked
coarse-grid regime supports local ignition without sustained front
propagation, consistent with lattice pinning."

Unsafe: "The GRIMOIRE equation cannot propagate fronts."

### 2. The Γ(U) boundary is untested.

All Step 4 tests used Γ(U)=0. The audit warns (Section 7) that non-local
or time-delayed Γ terms can break the gradient flow structure entirely.
No claim about the pinning boundary applies once Γ is non-trivial.

### 3. The coupling ratio threshold has not been analytically derived.

The D/dx² ≈ 1.5–2.0 boundary is an empirical observation from
numerical experiment. It has not been derived from the Peierls-Nabarro
theory or from the effective potential. An analytical derivation would
strengthen the result substantially.

### 4. 2D and 3D behavior is untested.

All Step 4 tests are 1D. The curvature penalty in higher dimensions
(Classical Nucleation Theory, audit Section 5) creates additional
barriers. The 1D pinning boundary is a lower bound on the constraint
in higher dimensions.

(D=0.12, α=π, dx=1.0), numerical experiments across two independent

propagation transition occurs at a coupling ratio D/dx² between 1.5
